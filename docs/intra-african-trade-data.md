## Product Composition (Intra-African Trade)

| Category | Share | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Manufactured goods | ~45% | Largest category for intra-African trade |
| Mineral fuels & oils | ~22% | Dominates total African exports but smaller share intra-Africa |
| Agriculture & food | ~18% | Cocoa, coffee, cereals, processed foods |
| Machinery & transport | ~10% | Driven primarily by South Africa |
| Other | ~5% | Ores, metals, precious stones, etc. |

Key product specifics:
- Non-monetary gold: 14.2% of intermediate goods exports (2022)
- Cocoa beans: $6B+ (2.1% of intermediate goods exports)
- South Africa's top exports: light oils, bituminous coal, chromium ores, electrical energy, goods vehicles
- Egypt's top exports: cement, gypsum, plastics, flour, phosphate fertilizers
- Ghana's top exports: gold (45.4%), petroleum (23.6%), cocoa beans (6.6%)

## Major Trade Corridors

| Corridor | Route | Significance |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| North-South Corridor | Durban (SA) - Lusaka (Zambia) | SADC backbone; carries copper, manufactured goods |
| Northern Corridor | Mombasa (Kenya) - Kampala (Uganda) - Kigali (Rwanda) | EAC's primary trade artery |
| Central Corridor | Dar es Salaam (Tanzania) - Kigali/Bujumbura | Alternative EAC route; growing DRC trade |
| Abidjan-Lagos Corridor | Abidjan - Accra - Lome - Cotonou - Lagos | ECOWAS flagship |
| Trans-Saharan Highway | Algiers (Algeria) - Lagos (Nigeria) | Trans-African Highway network |
| Walvis Bay Corridor | Namibia - Botswana - Mozambique | Inter-oceanic link (Atlantic to Indian Ocean) |

## Key Contextual Data Points

- Africa's total merchandise trade in 2024: **$1.5 trillion**
- Intra-African share: **14.4%** in 2024 (vs. EU 61%, Asia 59%, Latin America 15%)
- AfCFTA potential: Expected to boost intra-African trade by **$450 billion**
- Trade finance gap: **~$100 billion** annually
- COMESA-EAC-SADC Tripartite Free Trade Area (TFTA) entered into force July 25, 2024
- Manufactured goods, processed foods, automotive parts, and textiles now make up a rising share
